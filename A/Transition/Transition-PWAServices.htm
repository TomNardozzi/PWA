PWA Services

1)      Planning / Decision Support

a.      Respond to all client questions and help with decision making across virtually any topic that is at least remotely connected to personal finance.

b.     Perform cash flow planning, where appropriate, to ensure enough liquidity for major events and purchases.

c.      Help with goal feasibility and rationalization.

d.     Plan for and assist with financing of major purchases (houses, cars, etc.). Review tentative and final closing documents (closing disclosures, settlement statements, etc.).

e.      Identify refinancing opportunities.

f.        Help establish spending plans / budgets where appropriate.

g.      Assist with home purchase / home improvement planning by determining what is affordable and the purchases impact on other financial goals.

h.      Help clients find credit cards with rewards programs that benefit their spending habits.

i.        Recommend credit vehicles like Home Equity Lines of Credit (HELOC) and Home Equity Loans (HELs). Educate clients on the pros/cons of each.

j.        Help clients plan for how theyll pay for college for their children.

k.      Assist with debt payment plans (e.g. student loans) and with when to pay off loans ahead of schedule.

l.        Determine appropriate contributions to investment / savings vehicles to stay on track for financial goals.

m.   Help plan for cost of living changes, tax changes, and moving expenses when clients plan to move to a different state.

n.      Provide access to a financial aggregator showing all assets and liabilities, most updating in near real-time, and spending / budget tracker.

o.      Provide access to a secure portal for uploading/downloading/sharing documents with sensitive information.

p.     Track an action plan of recommended action items across all components of personal finance.

2)      Risk Management

a.      Calculate appropriate amounts and types of life insurance.

b.     Help with purchase of life insurance.

c.      Review property / casualty insurance (home/auto/umbrella) to ensure property is well-covered and liability risk in minimal.

d.     Address risk of disability via short-term and long-term disability insurance (group and/or individual).

e.      Help with long-term care planning and long-term care insurance, where appropriate.

f.        Assist with the selection of health insurance (group or market-based).

3)      Employee Benefits

a.      Assist with new hire and annual benefits enrollment and selection of optimal group benefits (medical, dental, vision, health FSA, dependent care FSA, HSA, life, disability, legal, LTC, etc.).

b.     Track employer equity awards (RSUs, Options, SSARs, etc.) and recommend optimal sale timing.

c.      For the self-employed, seek replacements for typical group benefits, including retirement plans (SEP, solo-401k, SIMPLE, etc.).

d.     Review benefits packages and total compensation for client job offers. Value packages to compare offers.

4)      Tax

a.      Monitor legislation changes and communicate updates to clients.

b.     Recommend tax optimization actions based on legislation or changes to the clients situation.

c.      Track capital gains / losses for managed and unmanaged investments.

d.     Assist with employee withholding settings at the Federal (W-4) and state level.

e.      Review client paystubs.

f.        Perform ongoing tax-loss (and sometimes tax-gain) harvesting in managed investments where appropriate.

g.      Optimize tax-location of investments (tax-deferred, taxable, and tax-free buckets) and investment withdrawals.

h.      Recommend Roth Conversions or other income acceleration techniques in client low-income years.

i.        Recommend expense timing to maximize tax deductibility (e.g. property taxes, business expenses, etc.)

j.        Calculate and recommend estimated tax payments based on supplemental wage underwithholding (bonuses, RSU vests), and/or safe harbors.

k.      Recommend Donor Advised Funds (DAFs) and help clients establish & fund them where appropriate.

l.        Project tax to be owed due to capital gain on the sale of an asset (e.g. rental property).

m.   Prepare / Review 83(b) elections upon receipt of employer stock when appropriate.

n.      Prepare and file Federal and State income tax returns and extensions (when necessary) for clients and their minor children.

o.      For clients that use an outside tax preparer, assist in gathering tax input documents annually and communicate with the preparer as necessary. Review prepared forms prior to filing.

p.     Incorporate private investment K-1s into personal taxes and help clients as appropriate questions of K-1 preparers.

q.     Assist with state-specific property tax refund returns (e.g. Minnesota).

r.       Help clients respond to IRS letters and inquiries.

s.      Prepare and file Gift Tax Returns and help clients tax-optimize gifting and charitable contributions.

t.       Help determine cost basis of inherited or gifted property or securities.

5)      Investments

a.      Manage client portfolios for retirement, non-retirement, and other specified goals to an appropriate, agreed-upon, risk-suitable, asset allocation.

b.     Monitor available investments to take advantage of new or reduced-fee funds.

c.      Incorporate clients employer requirements and restrictions on certain investments, asset classes, and institutions.

d.     Review portfolios for rebalancing at least quarterly and at major market moves.

e.      Keep clients informed about market performance especially around corrections and bear markets to ease concerns and stress.

f.        Keep clients focused on long-term returns for long-term goals, help them avoid the desire to attempt market timing, and hold talk me down off the ledge calls as needed.

g.      Remind clients of poor investment returns during good investment return times and vice versa.

h.      Recommend appropriate emergency fund amounts, accounts, and locations.

i.        Help clients manage and optimally distribute legacy holdings (employer stock, permanent life insurance policies, annuities, etc.).

j.        Help clients to earn a better return on cash than they do via their bank savings account (e.g. high-yield savings, money market funds, t-bills, etc.).

k.      Facilitate client deposits to investment accounts via ACH, check, or wire. Initiate transfers from bank accounts to investment accounts and from investment accounts to bank accounts for accounts with standing authority to do so.

l.        Pre-fill all investment forms requiring signatures and provide access to electronic signing tools as needed.

m.   Help with selling employer stock and enrolling in stock-based programs (e.g. ESPPs).

n.      Assist with the creation of 10b5-1 trading plans.

o.      Calculate expected return on investment for rental property purchases.

p.     Assist with consolidating accounts (e.g. 401k rollovers, HSAs from ex-employers, etc.).

q.     Recommend HSA providers that allow HSAs to be used for investment and manage those investments.

r.       Help clients apply for pledged asset lines using their brokerage accounts as collateral.

s.      Analyze and recommend participation level in deferred compensation plans.

t.       Review private investment opportunities to identify risks and potential returns.

u.     Vote client securities proxies upon request.

v.      Adjust asset allocations to address high exposures / correlation to certain asset classes (e.g. employer stock, pension exposure, rental real estate).

w.    Help to set up 529 college savings accounts for client children and calculate appropriate annual funding to stay on track for education goals.

x.      Open / manage UTMA investment accounts for client children.

6)      Retirement

a.      Keep clients on an appropriate glidepath toward their retirement goals via asset allocation change, spending adjustments, and retirement age feasibility review.

b.     Perform Monte Carlo analysis to estimate the probability of a successful retirement plan (i.e. not outliving money).

c.      Provide target annual funding by retirement savings vehicle (brokerage, IRA, Roth IRA, 401k, etc.) while in the asset gathering stage of life.

d.     Help determine retirement readiness as clients approach retirement age/goal.

e.      Determine sustainable withdrawal amounts and facilitate periodic withdrawal from tax-optimal accounts while in the asset distribution stage of life.

f.        Monitor employee retirement plan contributions through the year to ensure clients are obtaining the full allowable employer contribution.

g.      Assist with pension lump-sum vs periodic payment decisions.

h.      Manage client retirement investment portfolios, including employer-sponsored plans (e.g. 401k, 403b, etc.).

i.        Assist self-employed clients in acting as the plan administrator of their solo-401k plans. Help to file form series 5500 as necessary.

j.        Calculate Required Minimum Distributions (RMDs) from employer retirement plans, IRAs, and Inherited IRAs, and facilitate the RMD distributions.

k.      Help clients determine the best time to start drawing Social Security.

l.        Assist with Medicare enrollment and plan selection, where necessary.

m.   Review scenarios and adjust plans as client life events occur.

7)      Estate

a.      Act a bridge between estate planning attorney and client, translating client wishes into asks for their attorney and reviewing legal documents / recommendations to ensure they fulfill client wishes.

b.     Instruct clients on the types of documents to ask for from their attorney.

c.      Coach clients on Wills, Trusts, Powers of Attorney, and the Probate process so they have a basic understanding of how those things work and know what to expect at the death of a loved one.

d.     Ensure final estate plan is consistent with stated client wishes and types of assets/investments held.

e.      Document key client decisions (e.g. Memorandum of Understanding).

f.        Assist with applying recommended beneficiary settings on financial accounts and insurance policies.

g.      Periodically review beneficiary settings and note potential deficiencies for clients.

h.      Communicate with clients estate planning attorney / CPAs regarding suitability of and implementation of advanced estate planning / estate tax avoidance techniques.

i.        Work with clients executor and estate attorney following the death of a client to gather and distribute their assets.

j.        Assist with taking possession of inherited assets from deceased relatives / friends.

8)      Other

a.      Act as informal career counselor.

b.     Act as informal family mediator.

c.      Assist divorcing clients with tax optimized splitting of assets and implementation of that splitting per divorce decree.

d.     Help clients avoid scams by reviewing emails / letters received and discussing unprompted phonecalls. Provide recommendations on how to be more secure with sensitive information.

e.      Provide first line technical support on client financial websites (e.g. custodian, aggregator, etc.).

f.        Store copies of key client documents (e.g. tax filings, estate planning documents, life insurance policies, etc.).

g.      Provide ongoing support for any financial decision.
